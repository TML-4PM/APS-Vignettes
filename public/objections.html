agenticAI. It's ok!: Objection Handling Guide

A practical guide to anticipating and addressing common objections & concerns when implementing AI projects within the APS. This resource is designed to support project teams in navigating stakeholder discussions effectively.

We can’t use OpenAI, it’s not on our approved vendor list.

Reality Check:

They’re right — many agencies have not formally cleared OpenAI via API.

Proposed Counter:

Use Azure OpenAI hosted in Australian region (IRAP-ready, Microsoft-managed).

Backup Plan:

Default to GPT-3.5 or replace summarisation with keyword extraction + predefined template.

Before Holo-Org:

Briefing summaries were manually created, often inconsistently and under time pressure.

After Holo-Org:

AI-generated summaries pulled from approved content sources with consistent structure and auditability.

We don’t allow apps that read mailbox or calendar data.

Reality Check:

Mail/calendar access often requires admin consent and triggers privacy concerns.

Proposed Counter:

Use Graph API with least-privilege, user-scoped application permissions (no cross-user read).

Backup Plan:

Limit access to only calendar metadata (title, time, invitees) and exclude content fields.

Before Holo-Org:

Users had to manually search calendar history and email threads to prepare briefs.

After Holo-Org:

The agent automates the search and provides one-click summaries from allowable metadata.

Who approved this project or pilot?

Reality Check:

Government processes require formal ICT approval or innovation initiative alignment.

Proposed Counter:

Pre-align with the agency’s Digital/ICT business partner or pilot governance lead.

Backup Plan:

Submit a lightweight PoC request through an Innovation or Digital Transformation program.

Before Holo-Org:

Innovation attempts were derailed by lack of formal pathways.

After Holo-Org:

Pilots are embedded within sanctioned innovation channels with minimal friction.

This looks like shadow IT.

Reality Check:

Anything outside official governance or procurement is flagged as unapproved technology.

Proposed Counter:

Deploy using Microsoft 365-native components only — no new vendors or licenses.

Backup Plan:

Wrap the agent into a sanctioned pilot program with existing internal oversight.

Before Holo-Org:

Staff hesitated to try new tools due to fear of reprimand.

After Holo-Org:

Agent runs within the approved Microsoft ecosystem, documented and controlled.

Will this expose data inappropriately?

Reality Check:

Data exfiltration, especially to external APIs, is a top-tier concern.

Proposed Counter:

Keep all summarisation logic inside their tenant (Azure OpenAI only).

Backup Plan:

Create logs of all processed content and ensure only metadata, not message bodies, are handled.

Before Holo-Org:

Data exposure risk limited the adoption of AI-based tools.

After Holo-Org:

Localised processing and logging ensure complete transparency and audit readiness.
